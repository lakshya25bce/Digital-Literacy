#  Part B - Prevention Checklist

### Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students

As a Digital Ambassador, I have compiled these 8 actionable tips to help my peers at VIT Bhopal recognize and avoid common online threats.

1. **The UPI Golden Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send money. If a QR code or link asks for a PIN, it is a scam.
2. **Verify Financial Requests:** If someone asks for a payment or sends a refund/cashback QR code via WhatsApp or Telegram, call them to verify their identity before taking any action.
3. **Secure Your VTOP & Email:** Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your student email and any other academic portals that support it. Use an authenticator app rather than just SMS if possible.
4. **Inspect URLs Carefully:** Before logging into any university portal, ensure the URL is correct (e.g., `vitbhopal.ac.in`). Scammers often use "look-alike" domains to steal login credentials.
5. **Public Wi-Fi Hygiene:** Avoid accessing your bank account or sensitive academic data while connected to free public Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your connection.
6. **Strong, Unique Passwords:** Use a complex passphrase for your primary accounts. Avoid using easily guessable information like your name, registration number, or date of birth.
7. **Check App Permissions:** Regularly review your smartphone settings to see which apps have access to your SMS and Contacts. Malicious apps often use these to intercept OTPs.
8. **Keep Software Updated:** Ensure your laptop and smartphone have the latest security patches installed to protect against system-level vulnerabilities.

### How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know falls victim to a scam, act immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call this immediately for financial/UPI fraud to increase the chances of freezing the transaction).
