# Part A - Case Study

### Case Study: The Scholarship QR Code Scam

Aryan, a first-year student at VIT Bhopal, recently became a victim of UPI QR Code fraud, a common yet devastating financial cybercrime. It all started when he received a WhatsApp message from a profile with a convincing university-themed display picture. The message stated that Aryan was selected for a Digital Excellence Scholarship worth 5,000. To verify his bank account and receive the funds instantly, the attacker sent a QR code and urged Aryan to scan it within ten minutes before the offer would expire.

The crime involved clever social engineering. The attacker told Aryan to open his GPay app and scan the QR code, claiming that scanning was the only way to authorize money going into his account. Excited about the scholarship and feeling pressured by the countdown, Aryan scanned the code without carefully reading the screen. His UPI app asked him for his secret PIN, and in his haste, he entered it without realizing that the interface said Paying 5,000 instead of Receiving. As soon as he hit enter, his monthly pocket money was immediately debited, and the attacker blocked him.

This type of scam often targets college students who are familiar with digital payments but may not remember a basic rule a PIN is only needed to send money, never to receive it. For Aryan, the fallout went beyond losing 5,000 ; he experienced significant guilt and embarrassment that distracted him from studying for mid-terms for several days. This situation illustrates how scammers take advantage of trust and urgency, even with tech-savvy students.
