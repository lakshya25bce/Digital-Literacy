#  Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention

As a Digital Ambassador at VIT Bhopal, this module focused on researching modern online threats and creating resources to help my peers stay safe in the digital landscape.

## Part A: Cybercrime Case Study
I have documented a realistic scenario involving **UPI/QR Code Fraud**, a common threat facing college students in India. 
- **Scenario:** The "Scholarship" QR Code Scam.
- **Key Insight:** Scammers use social engineering and urgency to trick victims into entering their UPI PIN.
- [View Full Case Study](./casestudy.md)

## Part B: Stay Safe Online Checklist
I have created a targeted prevention guide with 8 actionable tips specifically for the student community.
- **Top Tip:** A UPI PIN is only required to **send** money, never to **receive** it.
- **Reporting Channels:** National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline **1930**.
- [View Prevention Checklist](./prevention-checklist.md)

## Part C: Project Report (Reflection)
While researching UPI fraud, I was most surprised by how scammers rely on "Social Engineering" rather than complex hacking. They create a sense of excitement or pressure that makes even tech-savvy students forget basic security rules. It was eye-opening to see how a simple QR code scan can bypass a person's common sense when they are in a hurry.

**Personal Habit Change:** As a result of this task, I will never scan a QR code for "receiving" money and will always verify the identity of a sender via a phone call before clicking on financial links. I will also actively educate my batchmates that a PIN is strictly for sending money.
